I am currently a physics and electrical engineering student at Stanford. I'm primarily interested in materials science, quantum hardware, and nanofabrication. Last summer, I investigated the stability and degradation mechanisms of GaN transistors at MACOM. This summer and during the school year, I work at the Congreve Lab, where I perform design perovskite LEDs using novel materials, device architectures, and fabrication techniques. Previously, I designed optical systems at SLAC for MAGIS-100, a quantum sensing experiment designed to detect dark matter and gravitational waves. In the past, I created a website about predicting the outcome of sporting events using statistical modeling. Here is a basic summary of how the model works and what tools I used to create it. Please reach out of we have similar interests or you are curious about anything I have worked on!
